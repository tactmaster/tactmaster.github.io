| 5 dl milk | 50 g fresh sweet yeast | 1 g saffron (2 packs) |
| ![Butter](/img/butter.jpg){:height="100px"} | ![Salt](/img/salt.jpg){:height="100px"} | ![Sugar](/img/sugar.jpg){:height="100px"} |
| 200 g butter | 1 teaspoon salt | 2 dl sugar |
| ![Special flour](/img/specialflour.jpg){:height="100px"} | ![Egg](/img/egg.jpg){:height="100px"} | ![Raisins](/img/raisins.jpg){:height="100px"} |
| 15 dl special flour | 1 beaten egg (for brushing) | Raisins |

Method:
1. Warm milk to 37 °C.
2. Crumble yeast into a bowl and "dissolve" in the milk.
3. Add saffron, butter, salt, sugar and flour.
4. Work the dough for 10 minutes with a machine until it is elastic and just comes away from the edges.
5. Cover and allow to rise for about 40 minutes.
6. Knead and split into 40 pieces.
7. Form each piece into a lussebulle and put on lined baking trays.
8. Cover and allow to rise for about 40 minutes.
9. Preheat oven to 240 °C.
10. Brush bullar with beaten egg and poke 2 raisins (deep!) into each.
11. Bake for 6-8 minutes.
